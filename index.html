Afterlives Festival 2026

Introduction

The Afterlives Arts Festival is coming to a library near you — it's free, fun and open to absolutely everyone. Come and explore life's biggest questions through art, creativity and community.

Try a hands-on craft workshop, wander through a stunning art installation made by people just like you, watch a film, experience virtual reality, discover eco-friendly ways to be remembered, or simply sit, reflect and connect with your neighbours over a shared meal. There's something for all ages, from creative activities for curious kids to thought-provoking evening events for adults.

No experience necessary. Just come as you are.

  • Newcastle Upon Tyne 8–9 May
  • Exeter 15–17 May
  • Redbridge 5–7 June

List of events

Pick an Afterlife

Have you ever wondered what different people around the world believe happens when we die? Come and find out — and make something amazing while you're at it! Drop in and get creative as we explore incredible afterlife ideas from different cultures and times throughout history, making your very own afterlife objects to take home. Feeling lucky? Have a go on our claw grab machine and let fate pick YOUR afterlife — what will you get?

Free to drop in. No booking needed. All ages welcome — little ones should come with a grown-up.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Batman (Naomi's Death Show)

Naomi will be performing her award-winning audience-interactive stage play, which explores the cultural exploitation of trauma, our fascination with true crime, and the power of communal grieving.

Naomi Westerman is an author, playwright, former anthropologist, and a death acceptance activist. Her book Happy Death Club was a best-seller in the UK, USA, and Canada.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

The Midnight Moth (Natural Burial Play Kits)

Join us during the reading of The Midnight Moth, a new lyrical mortality picture book, followed by a gentle interactive workshop that invites children and their families to design a simple, nature-based memorial ceremony for the Midnight Moth.

DEAD GOOD CIC is an arts-based social enterprise that aims to expand public imagination and creativity around mortality, rites of passage, legacy and remembrance.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Dead Good Film Club

Join us for a special screening from the Dead Good Film Club, encouraging conversations about death and dying that are often considered taboo. Expect great films, fascinating discussions and revealing insights from people working in areas as diverse as health care, undertaking, public services and the creative communities.

The Dead Good Film Club hosts screenings and events focused on death and dying to encourage conversation around this topic. Hosted by producer/funeral leader Andy Jones, in partnership with the Wayfaring Cinema and the Death Positive Library. It features films, panel discussions, and community events.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Immortelle

In Victorian times, people would place beautiful glass domes on graves, filled with precious objects, symbols and treasures to honour and remember their loved ones. Now that tradition is coming back to life and we want you to be part of it!

Newcastle-based Moving Parts Arts has worked with local artists in Newcastle, London and Exeter to create three stunning new artworks inspired by this forgotten custom. But these aren't just artworks to look at – they belong to you. Community workshops will bring together local people to contribute their own icons, symbols and treasured objects to each piece, making every Immortelle a living portrait of its community.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Resting Reef

Resting Reef is an award-winning death-care service that turns the ashes of those who have passed (human and pets) into beautiful memorial reefs that restore marine life.

Resting Reef will be showcased at the festival exhibition featuring their Memorial Reefs. The display will include an interactive space where visitors can learn more about Resting Reef’s mission, explore and handle miniature reef models, and take a moment to reflect on planning ahead. Guests can share ideas about future reef locations and imagine how these memorial ecosystems may grow over time.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

A Seat at the Table

Luna Arts is a non-profit organisation co-founded by artists Wendy Pye and Dagmara Rudkin, delivering creative projects and workshops that nurture wellbeing and help ease conversations about death and dying.

This reflective lunch invites participants to gather around a table to share a simple meal, with gentle creative moments that open space for thoughtful conversations around death, memory, and belonging.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Shroud Making

Luna Arts is a non-profit organisation co-founded by artists Wendy Pye and Dagmara Rudkin, delivering creative projects and workshops that nurture wellbeing and help ease conversations about death and dying.

This workshop invites participants to explore the resonance of cloth and textiles in their lives through conversation and hands-on activities, creating space for remembrance, connection, and reflection on the ancient practice of shrouding and personal choices around death.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Soul Paint

Soul Paint is a multi-award winning virtual reality experience by Sarah Ticho & Niki Smit, backed by behavioural scientists and researchers. Narrated by Rosario Dawson, this experience asks you the question: ‘Where are you feeling?’

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June

Symbols of Death

Moth Design for Life & Death. An initiative exploring the roles graphic designers can play in shaping end-of-life experiences. Predicated on the idea that confidently expressing ‘negative’ emotions helps us navigate grief and sadness and communicate empathy and loss.

A collective digital vocabulary of pictorial signs which articulate and communicate the nuances of death, dying and grief.

  • Newcastle City Library 8th May
  • Exeter Library 15th May
  • Redbridge Central Library 5th June
